# Business Case Text

Business Case: CS Operations Lead Technical Challenge

Context

You have just joined Ontop as our first CS Operations Lead. Our leadership team has been looking at high-level numbers, but we lack a deep understanding of two critical areas:
Why customers are churning.
Where our hidden growth opportunities lie.
You have been provided with a dataset (CS_Ops_Case_Study_Final.csv) containing 1,500 accounts with 12 months of MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) and TPV (Total Payment Volume) data, along with churn details and account age.Objective

Analyze the provided data and prepare an Executive Performance Report (3–5 slides or a 1-page dashboard) that helps the VP of Customer Success decide where to allocate resources for the coming year.

Part 1: The Data Analysis

Using the Google Sheets and/or Looker Stuio, please answer the following questions:
Revenue Health: What was the Net Revenue Retention (NRR) for the 12-month period?
Churn Driver Analysis:
Which "Churn Reason" has the highest impact on our bottom line?
Is there a specific month where churn spiked?
Customer Lifecycle: Is there a correlation between the Age_Months of an account and their likelihood to churn?
Efficiency Metric: Which cohort of customers is our most "efficient" (Highest TPV-to-MRR ratio)?
Part 2: The Deliverable

We are looking for a Clean, Aesthetic Financial Report. Your final submission should include:
Visual Dashboard: Professional-grade charts showing MRR trends, churn distribution, and TPV growth.
Key Insights: 3–5 "Executive Bullets" explaining what the data actually means for the business.
Action Plan: Based on your findings, what is the #1 priority for the CS team next quarter?
How You Will Be Evaluated

Your submission will be evaluated based on the following criteria:
Data Integrity: Did you correctly handle "N/A" values and account for the "bleed" in revenue before a customer officially churns?
Aesthetics: Is the report easy to read? Does it look like something that could be presented to a Board of Directors?
Strategic Thinking: Did you find the "hidden" trends, or did you only report on the surface-level numbers?
